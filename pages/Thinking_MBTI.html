The T-F dichotomy is designed to reflect a person's preference between two contrasting ways of making a judgment.

Thinking

A person may rely primarily on Thinking (T) to decide impersonally on the basis of logical consequences.They want to mentally remove themselves from the situation to examine the pros and cons objectively. They are energized by critiquing and analyzing to identify what’s wrong with something so they can solve the problem. Their goal is to find a standard or principle that will apply in all similar situations.

In Jung's and Myers' approaches, the term Thinking does not imply intelligence or competence, and the term Feeling is not to be confused with emotional. Intelligence and emotional expression are independent of psychological typology.

  • Basing conclusions on logical analysis with a focus on objectivity and detachment 
  • Apply logical reasoning
  • Use cause-and-effect analysis
  • Seek objective through
  • Decide using impersonal criteria
  • Focus on tasks
  • Provide a critique

Characteristics associated with people who prefer Thinking:

  • Analytical
  • Use cause-and-effect reasoning
  • Solve problems with logic
  • Strive for an objective standard of truth
  • Reasonable
  • Can be “tough-minded”
  • Fair—want everyone treated equally
